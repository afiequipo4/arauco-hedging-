Macroeconomic Variables

How the macro variables affect Arauco

Five indicators shape the FX outlook. Each is summarized with its recent trend and the concrete business impact for Arauco México, followed by interactive charts.

Inflation differential

Measures: Mexico vs US CPI — a PPP anchor.
Trend: Mexico eased to 4.45% (Apr); US rose to 3.8%. Gap ≈ +0.65 pp.
Impact: Local costs rise faster than USD inputs, eroding the natural hedge.

Interest rate differential

Measures: Banxico vs Fed — the carry-trade engine.
Trend: Banxico 6.50% (easing over); Fed 3.50–3.75%. ≈ +287 bps.
Impact: Props the peso up but is fragile; any unwind raises Arauco's cost of dollars.

GDP growth

Measures: Relative growth — a capital-flow driver.
Trend: Mexico −0.8% QoQ vs US +2.0% annualized.
Impact: Weak demand hits panel sales (~60% of revenue); US strength supports the Alacrán thesis.

Construction sector

Measures: The most direct wood-panel demand gauge.
Trend: −0.8% YoY (Feb) but five months of gains; +2.5% forecast 2026.
Impact: Contraction compresses MDF/PB/melamine demand.

Trade balance & remittances

Measures: The two largest USD inflows (~$700B/yr).
Trend: Surplus +$5.9B (Mar); remittances record but −18.7% in real MXN.
Impact: A USMCA shock would remove peso support, pushing USD/MXN higher.

USMCA review & energy

Measures: Structural / event risk.
Trend: Review opens July 1; Strait-of-Hormuz disruption lifts energy and urea prices.
Impact: Binary peso risk plus a direct cost shock to urea.

Risks & Structural Environment

What can move the peso against Arauco

Beyond the cyclical indicators, a set of structural and event-driven factors define Arauco México's FX risk. These are the forces the hedging strategy is built to withstand.

Dominant event risk · USMCA review (begins July 1, 2026)

The formal review can confirm continuation to 2042 or revise the agreement. Three paths: a clean renewal (peso strengthens on relief), an imperfect renewal (range-bound volatility), or a failed review that turns into serial annual reviews — which analysts estimate could depreciate the peso 5–15%. Because the Alacrán Project depends on tariff-free access to U.S. and Canadian plants, this is the single most important event for Arauco's 2026 FX exposure, and it calls for asymmetric, option-based protection.

Main risks for Arauco México

Carry-trade unwind

A BoJ hike, a Fed pause, or a trade shock could trigger a sharp, asymmetric USD/MXN spike — the tail the hedge must cover.

Sovereign downgrade

S&P cut Mexico's outlook to negative (May 13, 2026) on slow fiscal consolidation. A downgrade would force bond outflows and pressure the peso.

Sticky inflation + weak growth

A stagflationary undertone is structurally bearish for the peso medium-term, even while the carry trade masks it short-term.

Shifting exposure profile

The Alacrán Project raises USD revenue exposure, eroding the old natural hedge and making active hedging more important, not less.

Policy & structural backdrop

Monetary policy

The wide ~287 bps rate gap favors the peso near-term but is fragile. Banxico signalled its easing cycle is over; any combination of further cuts or a Fed pause could shift USD/MXN sharply.

Fiscal policy

The 2026 budget runs a 4.1% deficit with PEMEX support and welfare rigidity. Fiscal pressure is the primary medium-term peso risk and underlies the S&P outlook cut.

Socio-political

Sheinbaum manages inherited fiscal stress, the judicial reform, and U.S. tariff pressure via "quiet diplomacy"; Trump's trade posture keeps cross-border investment economics uncertain.

Regulation

Customs reform (Ley Aduanera, Jan 2026), anti-China tariffs, and tighter NOM environmental standards affect Arauco's import logistics; USMCA rules-of-origin tightening is the dominant regulatory risk.

Energy shock

The Strait-of-Hormuz disruption lifts U.S. inflation and has pushed urea up ~81% YoY — a direct cost shock to Arauco's resin feedstock and a driver of the larger hedge notional.

Nearshoring slowdown

17 consecutive months of declining fixed investment weaken the industrial-construction demand outlook, while making the Alacrán export bet more strategically valuable if USMCA holds.

FX Hedging Strategy

A combined derivatives approach

We recommend a low-cost collar — a long USD call (cap 18.00) funded by a short USD put (floor 16.85) — to hedge Arauco's six-month urea and methanol exposure. Net cost: MXN 1.16M (0.45% of notional); maximum protection in a bearish-peso crisis: MXN 29.6M.

Context & FX exposure

Arauco buys urea and methanol — both priced in USD — as inputs to resin and formalin production, while its revenue is predominantly MXN. This is a net USD payable exposure: when the peso weakens, the MXN cost of the same purchase rises.

Strategy design — the collar

A single forward would lock the rate but forfeit all upside; a naked call protects but drains cash. The collar combines both legs into one structure with bounded risk in both directions.

STEP 1

Buy a USD call @ 18.00

The protection leg — the right to buy USD at 18.00 if spot rises above it.

STEP 2

Sell a USD put @ 16.85

The funding leg — its premium funds most of the call.

STEP 3

Net payoff

Protected above 18.00, participating 16.85–18.00, giving up gains below 16.85.

Strategy analysis

When it helps

Any move above the 18.00 cap — especially a USMCA-driven spike. At 20.00, the collar saves MXN 29.6M.

When it costs

Below the 16.85 floor, Arauco forgoes better spot. At 15.50 the opportunity cost is MXN 20M — but this is also the cheapest unhedged outcome.

Residual risks

Counterparty (ISDA), volume basis (>20% deviation needs re-sizing), and mid-life mark-to-market.

Why still preferable

For 0.45% of notional, Arauco converts an unbounded tail risk into a bounded, planned cost.
